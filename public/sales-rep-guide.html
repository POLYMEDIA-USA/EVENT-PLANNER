Sales representative reference — lead capture and event engagement

As a Sales Rep, your primary role is to add leads (potential event attendees), scan QR codes at events for check-in, and log your interactions with leads. You see data scoped to your organization only.

Login URL: https://your-app-url.run.app

Getting Started

Your Account

Your Admin creates your account and assigns you to an organization. If you need account changes (password reset, role change, etc.), contact your Admin.

Signing In

  1. Navigate to the app URL.
  2. Enter your Email / Username and Password.
  3. Click "Sign In".

First Steps

  1. Go to the Dashboard and select an active event.
  2. Start adding leads in the Leads page.

Dashboard

Selecting an Event

The first thing you should do is select an active event. This scopes the Leads page to show leads associated with that event.

  1. On the Dashboard, you'll see available events listed.
  2. Click an event to select it.
  3. The selected event name appears at the top. Click "Change" to switch.

The stats cards show platform-wide metrics: Active Events, Total Leads, Invited, and Attended.

Leads

◉

Adding & Managing Leads

Scoped to your organization

Leads are potential attendees you want to track for the event. You see only leads from your organization.

Adding a New Lead

  1. Click "Add Lead".
  2. Fill in the form:
  • Full Name*The lead's full name
  • TitleJob title or position
  • Company*The lead's company name
  • Email*Primary email address
  • PhonePhone number
  • Alt EmailSecondary email address
  • Assigned RepSelect the sales rep responsible for this lead from a dropdown of all registered users

Input By is automatically set to your name and organization when you create a lead.

  1. Click "Create".

The lead is automatically associated with your organization and (if selected) the active event.

Editing a Lead

Click "Edit" next to a lead to update their details. Make changes and click "Update".

Deleting a Lead

Click "Delete" and confirm. This permanently removes the lead.

Searching

Use the search bar to filter leads by name, email, company, or phone number.

💡
Tip: Select an event on the Dashboard first to scope leads to that event. If no event is selected, you'll see all leads for your organization.

QR Scanner

⊞

Event Check-In

Scan attendee QR codes at the event

At the event, use the QR Scanner to check in attendees who have confirmed their RSVP.

Using the Camera

  1. Open the QR Scanner page on your phone or tablet.
  2. Click "Start Scanner". Allow camera access if prompted.
  3. Point your camera at the attendee's QR code.
  4. The system reads the code and displays the attendee's name, title, and organization.
  5. Optionally add interaction notes (e.g., "Met at booth 3, interested in product demo") and click "Save Notes".

Manual Check-In

If the camera can't read the QR code:

  1. Ask the attendee for their 4-character check-in code (e.g., K7PN — shown below the QR code in their confirmation email or RSVP page).
  2. Type the code in the manual entry field.
  3. Click "Check In".
📱
Best practice: Use your phone's rear camera in a well-lit area. Works on all modern browsers.

My Interactions

◎

Logging Interactions

You see only your own interaction logs

The Interactions page is a customer-centric note-taking hub. You see interactions for customers in your organization.

Viewing Interactions

  1. Select a customer from the left panel (search by name, title, company, or email).
  2. View their full interaction history — your notes, QR check-ins, and RSVP events.

Adding Notes

  1. Select a customer from the list.
  2. Type your notes in the "Add a Note" section.
  3. Click "Save Note". Your name and timestamp are recorded automatically.

Automatic Interactions

The system automatically logs interactions when you scan an attendee's QR code or when an attendee RSVPs.

📝
Log interactions promptly. Detailed notes help your team follow up effectively. Supervisors and Admins can see notes from all team members.

My Tasks (v0.5.0)

Your Admin or Supervisor can assign follow-up tasks to you. Open My Tasks to see tasks assigned to you with due dates and priorities.

  • Filter by: All, My Tasks, Overdue, Completed
  • Click "Mark Complete" when done
  • Overdue tasks are highlighted in red

New in v0.5.0

  • Lead Scoring — Leads now show hot/warm/cold scores in the table based on engagement.
  • Lead Source — Track where each lead came from (Manual, File Import, Referral, etc.).
  • Notes on Leads — Add internal notes when creating or editing leads.
  • Assigned Rep — See which rep is assigned to each lead.
  • Role-Based Dashboard — Your dashboard shows My Leads count, My Tasks summary, and Recent Interactions.
  • Calendar — Add events to your calendar with one click from the RSVP page.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Always select an event first. Go to Dashboard and pick your event — this ensures leads are properly associated.
  • Add leads as you meet them. The sooner you add a lead, the sooner your team can include them in event communications.
  • Include all contact info. Complete lead profiles (email, phone, company) make follow-up easier for the whole team.
  • Scan QR codes promptly. Check-in data helps your team know who actually showed up.
  • Log detailed interaction notes. Include what was discussed, the lead's interests, and any next steps.
  • Use your phone at events. The QR Scanner and Lead entry work great on mobile browsers.
